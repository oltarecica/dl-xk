# Patterns in Media Coverage of the War in Ukraine in Polarised Media

## Authors
- Anna Luntovska
- Marie Mousse
- Luna Baroni
- Oleksandra Povstiana

## Research Question
How do French and Chinese media frame the Russo-Ukrainian War?

The study compares:
- Le Monde
- China Daily

using NLP techniques.

## Data
- 2,870 articles
- 1,407 from Le Monde
- 1,463 from China Daily

## Methodology
- TF-IDF analysis
- BERT sentiment analysis
- VADER sentiment analysis
- TextBlob polarity and subjectivity

## Main Findings

### Le Monde
- Uses “war” most frequently
- Focuses on military and humanitarian dimensions

### China Daily
- Uses “conflict” and “crisis” more frequently
- Places greater emphasis on sanctions and diplomacy

## Sentiment Results

| Outlet | Average VADER Score |
|----------|----------|
| Le Monde | -0.27 |
| China Daily | +0.24 |

## Conclusion
The two outlets frame the war differently, reflecting distinct editorial and geopolitical perspectives.
