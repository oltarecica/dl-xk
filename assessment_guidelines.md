# 1. Term Poster

## Question

There are no restrictions on the question; you should select a topic that you find exciting. Addressing your question must include at least one prediction problem. Finally, if your question requires causal interpretation, you can use econometric models alongside deep learning models. Table A1 contains questions selected by students in previous years.

## Grading

Your term poster will be assessed according to several criteria, including the research question, a concise review of pertinent literature, dataset construction and cleansing, model selection and optimisation, result interpretation, and writing quality. The weighting of these criteria will vary based on the research question and the focus of the proposed paper. For instance, groups investigating questions requiring extensive data work will be evaluated accordingly, whereas those utilising existing datasets will have greater expectations placed on other criteria.

### Table 1: Grading Criterion

| Criteria   | Details                                      |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Question   | Substantive research question                |
| Background | Review a couple of related papers            |
| Model      | Model choice, modelling approach             |
| Data       | Collection, construction, cleaning           |
| Estimation | Optimisation, tuning parameters, statistics  |
| Results    | Interpretation, answer to the question       |
| Writing    | Clarity, organisation, no typos, referencing |

## Term Poster

You are free to structure the term poster as you wish, but it must include the key elements of an academic paper, presented in a concise and precise manner. This includes an introduction, a well-motivated research question, a background section drawing from relevant papers on the topic, a presentation of the data, a description of the methodology, the interpretation of results, and suggestions for further research directions. If necessary, refer to the various guides on "writing economics" available online.

Your term poster should not exceed **1500 words**, excluding captions, tables, references, the optional appendix, and the provided code.

## Tips

* Your poster must address a substantial research question rather than simply performing a predictive exercise.
* Emphasis will be placed on the appropriateness of your approach to the research question rather than the robustness of your results.
* Choose models based on their relevance to your research question and data structure; avoid benchmarking every available model.
* Ensure that everything you write directly addresses the research question. Aim for clarity; no extra credit will be awarded for unnecessary text.
* Using image data may present challenges due to dimensionality. We recommend conducting the analysis in a reduced-dimensional setting to test your idea.
* Avoid using Kaggle or similar off-the-shelf datasets unless they shed light on your research question.
* Large language models can be powerful learning tools, but make sure you fully understand the generated output and can assess its correctness. Simply copy-pasting generated code does not contribute to learning.
